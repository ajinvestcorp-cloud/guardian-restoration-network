Asbestos Abatement in Spokane, WA — Questions & Answers

Common questions from Spokane homeowners about asbestos abatement. If you don't see your question here, call us at (888) 625-0666 — we're available 24/7.

How do I know if my Spokane home has asbestos?
Homes built before 1980 in Spokane County frequently contain asbestos in insulation, floor tiles, ceiling tiles, and roofing materials. Testing is the only way to confirm — do not disturb suspected materials.
Is asbestos abatement required by law in Spokane?
Yes — asbestos removal in Washington requires licensed contractors following EPA and state OSHA regulations. Improper removal is illegal and dangerous.
How much does asbestos abatement cost in Spokane?
Costs vary based on the type and quantity of material. We provide a full assessment and transparent quote before work begins. Insurance may cover abatement triggered by a covered damage event.
Is asbestos dangerous if it's not disturbed?
Asbestos in good condition that is not friable (crumbling) poses minimal risk. However, any damage, renovation, or deterioration can release fibers — at which point abatement is required.
How long does asbestos abatement take in Spokane?
Most residential abatement projects complete in 1–3 days. Larger commercial projects or more extensive contamination may require additional time.
Can I stay in my Spokane home during asbestos abatement?
No — residents must vacate the affected area during abatement. We work efficiently to minimize disruption and confirm clearance before you return.
What happens to asbestos after removal in Spokane?
All asbestos-containing waste is sealed in approved containers and transported to licensed disposal facilities following Washington environmental regulations.
